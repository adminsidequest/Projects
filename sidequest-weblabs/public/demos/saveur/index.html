From the kitchen

Cooked close to home, plated without fuss

Every morning begins at the market. We build the menu around what the growers, fishers, and cheesemakers bring us that week, then let a few good ingredients do the talking. Slow stocks, fresh pasta rolled by hand, and a wood fire that runs from open to close.

The result is French at heart and neighborly in spirit. Refined, but the kind of room where you loosen your collar and stay for one more course.

    The chef

    Twenty years of fire, butter, and patience

    Camille trained across Lyon and the Basque coast before bringing that grounding home. Her cooking favors restraint over spectacle, a clean stock over a clever garnish, and the quiet confidence of an ingredient at its peak.

    "Good cooking is mostly listening. The market tells you what to make. You just try not to get in its way."

    Camille Marchand, on the philosophy at Saveur
